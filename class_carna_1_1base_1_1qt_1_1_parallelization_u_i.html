Instantiates.

Lets assume that \( J = \{ s_1, s_2, \dots, s_n \}\) is the job which we want to perform parallelized, with \( s_i \) as discrete steps, i.e. iterations. Lets further assume that we want to use \( m \) threads, which means that each thread will be performing \( \frac{ n }{ m } \) steps in average. Every time a certain thread has completed \( k \) steps, it will notify the ParallelizationUI instance by invoking ParallelizationUI::progress with \( k \) as argument. The instantiation we would require would take \( 1 \) for min and \( n \) for max.
